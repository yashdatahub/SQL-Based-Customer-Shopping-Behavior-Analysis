# 🍕 Pizza Store SQL Analysis Project

---

## 📘 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing pizza sales data using **SQL** to uncover business insights related to **revenue**, **customer preferences**, and **sales trends**.
The purpose is to understand how different pizzas, categories, and ordering patterns impact overall business performance.

---

## 🧭 Objectives

* Analyze pizza sales and revenue performance.
* Identify top-selling and high-revenue pizzas.
* Understand customer ordering behavior and peak hours.
* Compare performance across pizza categories and sizes.
* Support data-driven decisions for business growth.

---

## ⚙️ Tools & Technologies

* **Database:** MySQL
* **Language:** SQL
* **Tool:** MySQL Workbench

---

## 🧹 Data Preparation Steps

1. Imported CSV datasets into MySQL tables.
2. Verified and structured relational tables (orders, order_details, pizza, pizza_types).
3. Applied **Primary Key** and **Foreign Key** constraints.
4. Checked for missing or inconsistent data.
5. Ensured data integrity using SQL joins and validations.

---

## 🧠 Methodology

* **Data Loading:** Imported all datasets into structured SQL tables.
* **Exploration:** Performed exploratory queries to understand sales patterns.
* **Analysis:**

  * Calculated total revenue and order volume.
  * Identified top-performing pizzas and categories.
  * Analyzed hourly order distribution.
  * Evaluated daily and cumulative revenue trends.

---

## 💡 Observations & Insights

* Peak order times occur during lunch and evening hours.
* A small number of pizzas contribute to a large portion of total revenue.
* Certain categories (like Classic or Chicken) perform better than others.
* Larger pizza sizes generate higher revenue per order.
* Customer demand shows consistent daily ordering patterns with slight variations.

---

## 🚀 Challenges Faced

* Understanding relationships between multiple tables.
* Writing efficient JOIN queries across datasets.
* Handling aggregation with GROUP BY and SUM.
* Avoiding common SQL mistakes (joins, grouping errors).
* Optimizing queries for better performance and clarity.

---

## 🏁 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how **SQL-based analysis** can provide valuable insights into business performance.
By analyzing pizza sales data, we can identify trends, optimize menu offerings, and improve decision-making.
The study highlights the importance of **data-driven strategies** in improving revenue and customer satisfaction.
